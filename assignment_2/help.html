Drawing tools

Some common tools that are nearly universal among all drawing softwares are the standard brush tool that can often be right clicked to reveal many different brush options.
The eraser tool that does exactly what it says it does; however the opacity and size of the eraser can be adjusted through various means.

The layers selection that is often found somewhere on the right side of the workspace (it is good practice to label and utilize your layers)

Masks, while somewhat more advanced are used to manipulate layers they are attached to in order to hide/reveal certain parts of the layer, add effects, warp certain aspects, as well as many other things.

This concludes the brief tool tutorial, and many other resources can be found online.
